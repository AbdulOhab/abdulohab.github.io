About the Project#

Byapon (ব্যাপন) is one of Bangladesh’s most beloved youth science magazines, and I had the incredible honor of developing their digital platform. This project wasn’t just a development task for me — it was a passion project. Being able to contribute to a platform that nurtures young scientific minds in Bangladesh felt deeply meaningful. I learned so much while building this project, and it’s an experience I will carry with me for the rest of my life.

The Challenge#

Byapon needed a complete digital transformation. The existing platform was outdated and couldn’t handle the growing community of young science enthusiasts. They needed:

  • A modern, responsive platform that works seamlessly on all devices
  • Easy content management for publishing science articles
  • Event management for science festivals and workshops
  • A Q&A section where curious minds can ask science questions
  • Multi-category content organization (Physics, Biology, Astronomy, Technology, etc.)

Key Features I Built#

Content Management System#

A robust CMS that allows the Byapon team to publish articles across multiple categories including Physics, Mathematics, Technology, Chemistry, Biology, Astronomy, Health, Environment, and Science Fiction.

Digital Magazine Store#

An integrated e-commerce system where readers can purchase digital and physical copies of the bi-monthly magazine. Each issue is priced affordably (30-50 BDT) to ensure science education remains accessible.

Event Management#

A dedicated section for managing and promoting science events.

Science Q&A Platform#

An interactive section where young readers can ask science questions and get detailed, educational answers.

Publication Management#

A comprehensive library system allowing users to access archived content and resources. One of the core features of the platform is the digital magazine store. Byapon publishes a bi-monthly science magazine that has been inspiring young minds since its inception.

Tech Stack#

Backend: - PHP, Laravel

Frontend: - Blade Templates, Tailwind CSS, DaisyUI, Alpine.js, Vite

Challenges & Solutions#

1. Rich Text Editor Integration (Summernote)#

The Problem: Integrating Summernote WYSIWYG editor with Laravel Blade templates was one of the most frustrating challenges. The editor needed to:

  • Handle Bengali (Unicode) text properly
  • Support image uploads to the server
  • Work seamlessly with Livewire components
  • Not conflict with other JavaScript libraries

The Solution:

  • Created custom Vite aliases to resolve jQuery path issues
  • Implemented custom image upload callback with CSRF token handling
  • Added paste event handlers to clean incoming content

2. JavaScript Library Integration problems in Blade Templates#

JavaScript libraries were not properly integrated into Blade templates, causing unexpected behavior and functionality issues.

Impact & Results#

Since launching the new platform:

  • 10,000+ monthly active readers
  • 150 science articles published monthly
  • Multiple science events successfully promoted
  • Increased magazine subscriptions through the digital store
